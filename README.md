# Story of Shahrzad

## 📌 Overview  
This project presents a human centered data story that explores the emotional and psychological impact of political unrest through the perspective of an international student.  

The narrative follows *Shahrzad*, an Iranian student living abroad, who experiences the consequences of political events in her home country from a distance. The project highlights how data points such as internet shutdowns and reported casualties are not just abstract figures, but lived experiences that deeply affect individuals, identity, and human connection.  

The goal of this project is to humanize data and demonstrate how storytelling can bridge the gap between statistics and real-world impact.

## Objective  
- Transform a complex socio-political issue into a **compelling narrative**  
- Emphasize the **human impact behind data**  
- Design a story that is accessible to a **broad, non-technical audience**  
- Apply storytelling techniques to create **emotional engagement and awareness**

---

## Story Format  
The project is designed as a **visual scrollytelling narrative**, structured like a digital storyboard or interactive webpage.  

The story unfolds in stages:
1. A calm academic life abroad  
2. Gradual disruption through loss of communication  
3. Rising fear and uncertainty  
4. Emotional impact of reported events  
5. A transition toward global awareness and solidarity  

This format allows the audience to **experience the emotional progression** alongside the character, rather than passively consuming information.

## Design Iterations  

### Initial Concept  
The first version focused heavily on political facts and statistics, including protest data and internet shutdowns. While informative, the narrative felt distant and lacked emotional engagement, resembling a traditional report rather than a story.

### Adding Human Perspective  
In the second iteration, the focus shifted to Shahrzad’s lived experience*  
Data was introduced only when it directly affected her life, creating a clear connection between:
- Political decisions  
- Personal consequences  

This significantly improved relatability and emotional depth.

### Final Version  
The final iteration strengthened the emotional arc by incorporating elements of global response and solidarity. 

Rather than ending in isolation, the story concludes with a sense of:
- Shared humanity  
- International awareness  
- Collective responsibility  

This reframes the narrative from one of suffering to one of connection and hope.

---

## Target Audience  
- General public with limited data literacy  
- Human rights advocates  
- Individuals unfamiliar with the personal impact of global political events  

The project intentionally avoids complex visualizations, using simple visuals and selective data to ensure clarity and accessibility.

---

## Key Takeaways  
- Data becomes more impactful when placed within a human context
- Storytelling enhances understanding and emotional engagement  
- Visual pacing can guide audience perception and interpretation  
- Effective communication requires balancing data, narrative, and design

---

## Reflection  
This project demonstrates that political crises are not isolated or abstract—they extend into the everyday lives of individuals across the world.  

By combining storytelling with carefully integrated data, the narrative emphasizes that behind every statistic are real people experiencing fear, uncertainty, and hope.  

Ultimately, the project highlights the importance of global awareness and solidarity in shaping understanding and driving meaningful attention to critical issues.
